About Me



tldr: Taco Aficionado. Military Vet. World's Okayest Mom. Hooked on learning.

  • 2004-2014

    San Diego

    After college I began my career in legal where I worked both at an intellectual property law firm and an in-house legal department for a large tech company in San Diego. I was also in the USCG Reserves doing law enforcement and search & rescue and developing a passion for kick-ass street style tacos.

  • May 2014

    Silicon Valley

    After popping out a couple kids, we uprooted the crew and moved to Santa Clara, in the heart of Silicon Valley. My husband joined a start-up and I started working in the in-house legal department of one of the most admired companies in the world.

  • 2014-2018

    The Legal Burn out

    I was a commercial contracts negotatior working a full time job, raising (now 3) little kids, and trying to juggle parenthood in the bustling tech scene that is Silicon Valley. It was hard. I was constantly stressed out. I didn't like what I was doing anymore and was starting to feel a panic not knowing how to change it. I was in a senior position and doing anything new would mean I would have to completely start over from scratch and that was SCARY. I had been in legal my entire professional career, that was all I knew.

  • June 2018

    Raleigh, North Carolina

    We had had enough of the craziness of Silicon Valley so we moved back to the east coast to Raleigh, NC. I started yet another job at an in-house legal department for a video game company. I was overworked, overwhelmed, stressed out, missing my kids, and feeling completely terrified that I just felt *stuck*. My mental health was pushed to the brink and I did something I had never done before- I quit. I quit without having another job lined up. But suddenly, I FELT THE FREEDOM to DO SOMETHING ELSE. Ultimately, that job was the cataylst to me deciding to take a leap in the deep end and switch careers to web development. I guess I needed to hit rock bottom in order to find the courage to change.

  • September 2018

    A New Career

    Once I committed to switching my career to web development I was ALL IN. So far I have taken courses by Frontend Masters, Udemy, FreeCodeCamp, Thinkful, the list goes on. I've learned HTML5, CSS3, Bootstrap, Keyframes, Animations, JavaScript and the learning will never stop. I've learned so much in the short amount of time I have been coding and I am completely hooked and ready to conquer anything. #MomsCANcode.

  • 2019
    TBD...

The Swaddled Sprout

A local resource for all things pregnancy and postpartum.

The Swaddled Sprout was the brain child of my girlfriend and I who had a really hard time after giving birth to our first children. We felt very alone and isolated and wished we had a greater support system postpartum. We spent hundreds of hours going over every detail of our Swaddled Sprout resource and we launched the site in April of 2013, 7 months after my second baby was born. We offered breastfeeding classes, new mom support groups, blogs on everything from cloth diapering to post partum depression. It was my passion project for a very long time and we helped many local moms navigate parenthood.

  • Date: Launched April 2013
  • Logo Design by Brooke Cheek
